0x13F05 0x13F06
In both cases, the lines "extend beyond each other". If the glyphs are accurate, then a different way should be found to phrase the difference between 0x13F05 and 0x13F06 (and preferably not by counting lines). If the orientation of the angles (sloping "forward" or sloping "backward") is the difference, then the mirroring control could have been 
used instead of duplicating the approximate shape.
<p>
In Unicode 18, the descriptions will avoid counting, and the description of 0x13F06 will differ from that of
0x13F05 in having "square". I suspect that if more tokens in actual inscriptions are compared, 
the aspect ratio will turn out to be arbitrary, on a continuum from much wider than tall to approximately 
as wide as tall, and possibly some taller than wide.
In other words, here we may have another case of fetishizing normalized fonts.
<p>
